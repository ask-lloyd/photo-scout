# Golden Hour Clear

## What Creates This Opportunity

Golden hour under clear skies is the most reliable photography lighting condition. With cloud cover below 30%, the sun's low angle sends warm, directional light across the landscape without obstruction. High visibility (>15 km) means minimal atmospheric haze, keeping shadows clean and colors true. Moderate humidity (20–65%) provides just enough atmospheric scattering for warmth without muddying the scene.

This rule scores lower weight (0.7) than more dramatic events because clear golden hour is common and predictable. It won't produce the jaw-dropping sky of a storm break or epic sunset, but it consistently delivers beautiful, workable light.

## What to Look For

- **Long shadows** that reveal texture in landscapes, architecture, and portraits
- **Warm color temperature** shifting from ~3500K to ~2500K as the sun approaches the horizon
- **Clean, directional sidelight** ideal for showing three-dimensional form
- **A clear horizon** ensuring the golden period lasts its full duration

## Sample Shots

- Portraits with warm rim lighting and soft fill from open shade
- Landscapes emphasizing texture — sand dunes, plowed fields, rocky terrain
- Architecture with long shadows creating geometric patterns
- Backlit subjects with lens flare for an ethereal look

## Tips

- Golden hour is roughly 1 hour after sunrise and 1 hour before sunset, but varies by latitude and season
- Use a warm white balance (Daylight or Shade) to lean into the golden tones, or cool it down for a more naturalistic look
- Sidelight (sun at 90° to the camera) maximizes texture; backlight creates drama
- This is ideal portrait light — soft enough to flatter skin, directional enough to sculpt features
- Reliable conditions make this the best time to revisit compositions and refine technique
