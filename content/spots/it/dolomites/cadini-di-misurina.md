# Cadini di Misurina Viewpoint

The most dramatic pinnacle viewpoint in the Dolomites — a row of cathedral-like rock spires (the "Cadini" group) seen from a narrow rock ledge along the Bonacossa via ferrata trail. The ledge composition (popularized into a meme on Instagram, but no less stunning in person) faces southwest, so the spires light up beautifully at golden hour and sunset.

**Critical for your trip**: the Tre Cime toll road (SP49b) — the easiest access — is closed late autumn through roughly late May. If the road is still closed during your visit, this spot requires a far longer hike from Misurina or Auronzo. Confirm road status with Rifugio Auronzo before committing.

## Best Compositions

- **Classic ledge viewpoint** — From the well-known ledge ~25 min hike from Rifugio Fonda Savio, 16-24mm f/11 with the foreground rock and trail leading the eye toward the row of spires. Best at golden hour and the 20 min after sunset.
- **Telephoto pinnacle isolation** — 70-200mm picks out individual spires against sky. Try silhouettes against the post-sunset color band.
- **Sunrise alpenglow** — Pinks/oranges hit the west face of the spires from behind the photographer ~5-10 min before official sunrise. Less crowded than sunset.
- **Astro with spires as foreground** — In summer, the Milky Way core sits south-southwest. 14-24mm f/2.8 ISO 3200 25s composes the galactic core above the pinnacle row.

## Tips

- The viewpoint is a narrow exposed rock platform with a serious drop on three sides. **Do not crowd, do not lean over the edge.** Multiple fatal accidents have occurred here.
- 1.5–2 hr round-trip hike from Rifugio Auronzo via Rifugio Fonda Savio. Bring headlamp for sunrise/sunset.
- Sturdy boots, layers, and a wind shell — temperatures drop fast at 2,300m even in summer.
- If the toll road is closed, plan an alternative — Lago di Sorapis (also closed early season for snow) or Lago di Federa are nearby substitutes.
