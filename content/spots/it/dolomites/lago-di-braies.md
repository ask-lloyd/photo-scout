# Lago di Braies (Pragser Wildsee)

The most photographed lake in the Dolomites — a turquoise-emerald alpine lake cradled beneath the sheer 2,800m wall of the Croda del Becco. The wooden boathouse on the north shore, with its row of weathered rowboats, is the iconic foreground that has launched a thousand Instagram posts. The lake faces roughly south-southwest, so the wall behind the boathouse catches first light at sunrise and warm side-light through golden hour.

Mornings are everything here. Arrive before sunrise to claim a clean spot — by 9 AM the boathouse area is shoulder-to-shoulder. Mid-summer (Jul–Sep) requires a paid timed-entry vehicle reservation; off-season (Oct–early Nov, Jun) is far calmer.

## Best Compositions

- **Boathouse with mirror reflection** — From the dock 20m south of the boathouse, shoot at 24-35mm f/8 with the boats fanning out as a leading line into the lake. Calm water before 7 AM gives a perfect mirror.
- **Wide alpine establisher** — Walk the western trail 5-10 min to a small rocky shoreline. 16-24mm f/11 captures the entire Croda del Becco wall above the lake.
- **Boathouse detail tight** — Isolate two or three weathered rowboats with the 70-200mm at f/4-5.6 from the eastern shore. Compresses the boathouse against the cliff face.
- **Drone-style elevated angle (no drone needed)** — Hike 25 min up the Hochalpenkopf trail on the east side for a tree-framed overhead view of the entire lake.

## Tips

- A circular polarizer is essential — it cuts the surface glare and reveals the green-turquoise color of the deep water.
- The boathouse roof is in deep shadow until ~9 AM in summer; bracket exposures or use graduated ND if you want detail there.
- Drones are **banned** inside the protected nature park boundary (entire lake area).
- Late October dusts of fresh snow on Croda del Becco with the lake still unfrozen is a magical 1-2 week window.
