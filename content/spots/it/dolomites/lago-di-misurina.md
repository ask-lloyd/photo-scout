# Lago di Misurina

A 1.4 km perimeter alpine lake sitting at 1,754m, ringed by hotels on its eastern shore and looking west across calm water to the Sorapiss massif and (from the right angles) the back side of the Cadini di Misurina pinnacles. Easier to photograph than Braies — no entry restrictions, free roadside parking, and the western shore stays mostly empty even in peak season.

The lake faces west, so reflections are best in the evening. Calm conditions in the hour before sunset and the half-hour after tend to be reliable. In winter the lake freezes solid and becomes a dramatic skating-rink foreground.

## Best Compositions

- **Sorapiss reflection at sunset** — From the western shore boardwalk, 24-35mm f/11 with the Sorapiss massif and its golden-hour glow mirrored in the lake.
- **Hotel Misurina classic postcard** — From the south end pullout, 24-50mm framing the iconic yellow Grand Hotel against the Cadini and Sorapiss peaks reflected in the foreground water.
- **Cadini back-side telephoto** — From the eastern shore, 70-200mm picks out the jagged Cadini pinnacles peeking over the ridge to the south.
- **Blue hour with hotel lights** — 30 min after sunset, the Grand Hotel windows light up against the deepening blue sky and still-reflective water — strong warm/cool contrast at f/8 with 8-15s exposures.

## Tips

- This is the staging area for the Cadini di Misurina hike — combine the two for one trip.
- A light wind kills reflections instantly; check forecasts and aim for sub-5 mph wind windows.
- Toll road SP49b to Tre Cime starts here (closed late autumn through late May — the road is closed for your trip).
- Café Genzianella on the southwest shore is a good fallback if weather collapses; long-lens shots from inside are still possible.
