# Cortina d'Ampezzo

The "Queen of the Dolomites" — a wealthy alpine resort town sitting in a wide valley ringed 360° by some of the most photogenic peaks in the range: Tofane to the west, Cristallo to the northeast, Sorapiss to the south, and Pomagagnon directly above the town. The 75m-tall bell tower of Santa Maria Assunta in the town centre, with snow-capped peaks rising directly behind it, is the iconic Cortina town shot.

This is your basecamp for the trip — and a worthwhile subject in its own right at golden hour, blue hour, and after dark when the town lights up against the dark mountain mass.

## Best Compositions

- **Bell tower with Pomagagnon backdrop** — From Corso Italia (the main pedestrian street) looking north, 50-85mm f/5.6-8 compresses the tower against the cliff face. Blue hour with town lights warm and sky deep blue is the killer time.
- **Town panorama from Tofana cable car (Col Drusciè)** — The first stage of the Freccia nel Cielo cable car gets you to a viewpoint at ~1,778m looking down on the town with peaks behind. 24-50mm f/8-11. Best at sunset when the town stays lit and the peaks catch warm side-light.
- **Pomagagnon dawn alpenglow** — From any north-facing window or street, the cliff wall above Cortina catches dramatic pink-orange light 10 min before sunrise. 70-200mm isolates the wall.
- **Night street scene** — Corso Italia after dark with stone facades, lit shop windows, and the dark mass of mountains looming above. 35-50mm f/2.8, handheld at ISO 1600-3200 if your A7R V is along.

## Tips

- Cortina is the staging point for almost every other Dolomites shoot — Misurina is 25 min east, Lago di Federa 30 min south, Falzarego/Lagazuoi cable car 20 min west.
- Restaurants book up in high season; reserve dinner before evening shoots run long.
- The Mietres and Faloria cable cars are alternatives to Tofana for elevated town views.
- April/May and Oct/Nov shoulder seasons have many cable cars and high mountain roads closed for maintenance — check before planning ascents.
