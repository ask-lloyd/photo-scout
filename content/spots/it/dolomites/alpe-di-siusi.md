# Alpe di Siusi (Seiser Alm)

Europe's largest high-altitude meadow — 56 km² of rolling alpine grassland sitting at 1,800–2,000m, ringed by the cathedral spires of the Sassolungo / Sassopiatto and the Schlern massif. Endless leading lines from old wooden hay barns, dirt tracks, and grass ridges that point straight at the peaks. This is the panorama capital of the Dolomites.

Vehicle access is restricted in summer (9 AM – 5 PM closed to private cars) which means most photographers either drive up before 9, take the Seis cable car, or stay overnight at one of the alm hotels. Staying up top is the move — you get sunrise, sunset, and astro all without a commute.

## Best Compositions

- **Sassolungo at sunrise from Compatsch ridge** — 24-35mm f/11 with a wooden hay barn or dirt track in the foreground pointing east toward the Sassolungo group. Pink-orange alpenglow hits the peaks 5-10 min before sun-up.
- **Schlern massif at golden hour** — From the trail toward Saltria, a 35-50mm at f/8 frames the dramatic cliff-face of the Schlern with rolling meadow leading in.
- **Lonely larch + barn detail** — Late September larches turn gold; a 70-200mm picks them out against meadow or peaks.
- **Astrophotography** — Bortle 2-3 sky here. Milky Way arches over the Sassolungo from May through August. Shoot 14-24mm f/2.8, 20s, ISO 3200-6400.

## Tips

- A solid tripod and a wide aperture lens (f/2.8 or wider) make this spot punch above its weight for astro.
- Wind exposure is significant — bring a windbreaker even in July.
- The meadow is private grazing land — stay on marked trails.
- Sunrise in summer ~5:50 AM; sunset ~9 PM. Plan for short nights if shooting astro.
- Florian or Saltria areas are ideal staging points if you want fewer people than Compatsch.
