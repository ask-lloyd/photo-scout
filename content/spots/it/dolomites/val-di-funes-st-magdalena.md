# Val di Funes — St. Magdalena & San Giovanni in Ranui

Possibly the most painterly scene in the Alps: a tiny onion-domed church standing alone in a green meadow, with the jagged Odle (Geisler) peaks spiking up behind. Two churches deliver the shot — **San Giovanni in Ranui** (the famous solitary white chapel, private property, viewable only from the official fenced viewpoint) and **St. Magdalena church** (on a hill above the village, with a wider valley composition).

The Odle range faces east, so it catches dramatic alpenglow at sunrise (peaks turn orange-pink while the meadow is still in shadow) and a lovely warm side-light at sunset. Late September through mid-October the larches turn gold for an even richer color palette.

## Best Compositions

- **Ranui chapel + Odle classic** — From the official Ranui viewpoint, 70-100mm f/8 compresses the chapel against the peaks. Time it for sunrise alpenglow on the wall.
- **St. Magdalena village panorama** — From the marked viewpoint above the village (10 min walk from the parking lot at Zannes), 24-50mm f/8-11 frames the church, scattered farmhouses, meadow, and full Odle range.
- **Reverse golden-hour side light** — Sunset from the St. Magdalena viewpoint paints the church and farmhouses warm while the Odle wall goes into cool shadow — strong color contrast.
- **Telephoto isolation** — 200-400mm to pick out a single farmhouse or hay barn against the rock wall for graphic, near-abstract compositions.

## Tips

- Respect the property — Ranui chapel is on a working farm; do not climb the fence or trespass for a "closer" shot. The fenced viewpoint is the shot.
- Sunrise here in summer is ~5:45 AM; arrive 30 min early.
- An 82mm CPL helps deepen the meadow greens and saturate the sky.
- The road through Funes is narrow and twisty; budget 1.5 hr from Cortina, 45 min from Bolzano.
