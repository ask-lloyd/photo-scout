# Enchanted Rock State Natural Area

Enchanted Rock is a massive pink granite dome rising 425 feet above the surrounding Hill Country terrain. The exposed rock surface provides an unobstructed 360-degree view from the summit, making it equally compelling for sunrise, sunset, and astrophotography. The ancient granite batholith covers roughly 640 acres and is one of the largest such formations in the United States.

The surrounding landscape of oak woodlands and grasslands shifts dramatically with the seasons, from lush green in spring to golden browns in fall and winter. On clear moonless nights, the dark skies here reveal the Milky Way core with striking clarity, and the smooth granite summit serves as a natural tripod platform.

## Best Compositions

- **Milky Way arching over the dome** — Use a 14-24mm at f/2.8, ISO 3200-6400, with 15-20 second exposures. Position yourself on the lower slopes to include the dome silhouette against the galactic core.
- **Sunrise panorama from the summit** — Shoot with a 24-70mm at f/8, stitching 5-7 frames to capture the full 360-degree view as warm light sweeps across the Hill Country.
- **Vernal pools on the rock surface** — After rain, shallow depressions fill with water creating natural mirrors. Use a 16-35mm at f/11 to capture reflections of clouds or sunset color.
- **Layered Hill Country ridges at sunset** — Face west from the summit with a 70-200mm at f/8 to compress the layered blue ridgelines into an atmospheric telephoto landscape.

## Tips

- The park frequently reaches capacity on weekends and holidays. Arrive before the gate opens or make day-use reservations online well in advance.
- For astrophotography, plan around new moon phases and check the park's overnight access policies, as standard day-use hours may not cover late-night shooting.
- The hike to the summit takes 30-45 minutes; carry all gear you need since there is no opportunity to return to the car quickly.
