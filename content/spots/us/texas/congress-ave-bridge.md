# Congress Avenue Bridge

The Congress Avenue Bridge in downtown Austin is home to the largest urban bat colony in North America, with an estimated 1.5 million Mexican free-tailed bats roosting beneath the bridge from March through November. Each evening near sunset, the bats emerge in a swirling dark cloud against the sky, creating one of Austin's most unique photographic spectacles. The bridge also offers excellent views of the downtown skyline and Lady Bird Lake.

Beyond the bat emergence, the bridge and its surroundings provide strong urban photography opportunities. The south-facing view captures the downtown skyline reflected in Lady Bird Lake, and the Ann W. Richards Congress Avenue Bridge itself is an architecturally clean subject. During blue hour, the city lights create a warm glow against the deep blue sky.

## Best Compositions

- **Bat emergence against sunset sky** — Position yourself on the east side of the bridge or along the lakeshore trail. Use a 70-200mm at f/4, ISO 1600-3200, with a shutter speed of 1/500+ to freeze individual bats in the swirling column.
- **Downtown skyline reflection in Lady Bird Lake** — From the south bank of the lake, use a 24-70mm at f/8 during blue hour with a 10-15 second exposure to capture smooth water reflections of the illuminated skyline.
- **Bridge architecture at blue hour** — Shoot from the trail beneath the bridge with a 16-35mm at f/11, using the bridge's underside as a geometric frame for the skyline beyond.
- **Kayakers on Lady Bird Lake with skyline** — During golden hour, use a 70-200mm at f/5.6 to capture kayakers and paddleboarders on the lake with the warm-lit skyline as backdrop.

## Tips

- Bat emergence timing varies but generally occurs around sunset from March through October. Check local bat hotlines or the Austin American-Statesman's bat observation page for current timing.
- The lakeshore trail east of the bridge offers a better vantage point than standing on the bridge itself, as you can see the bats streaming out against the sky rather than looking straight down.
- Street parking is limited downtown. Consider parking south of the river or using rideshare to avoid circling for spots during peak viewing times.
