# Mount Bonnell

Mount Bonnell is one of Austin's highest points, offering a commanding west-facing overlook of Lake Austin, the surrounding Hill Country, and the city skyline. The 102-step stone staircase leads to a series of limestone ledges and viewing platforms perched above the Colorado River, making it a classic spot for sunset photography.

The overlook provides clear sightlines to the west, where the sun drops behind the layered hills beyond Lake Austin. In spring, wildflowers dot the surrounding hillsides, and the live oaks frame compositions naturally. The mix of water, greenery, and distant hills creates a layered landscape that rewards both wide-angle and telephoto approaches.

## Best Compositions

- **Sunset over Lake Austin** — Use a 24-70mm at f/8 to capture the sun setting behind the western hills with the lake winding below. Include overhanging oak branches as a natural frame.
- **City skyline from the north overlook** — Walk past the main viewpoint to find angles that include the downtown skyline to the southeast. A 70-200mm at f/5.6-8 compresses the cityscape against the foreground trees.
- **Boat trails on Lake Austin** — During golden hour, use a 70-200mm to capture boats leaving wakes on the glassy lake surface, creating dynamic leading lines.
- **Panoramic stitch from the summit** — Shoot a multi-frame panorama with a 35-50mm lens at f/11 to capture the full sweep from the Hill Country to downtown.

## Tips

- The stairs can be crowded near sunset, especially on weekends. Arrive 60-90 minutes early to claim a good position and explore different vantage points.
- The park closes at 10 PM. Plan your blue hour and twilight shooting accordingly.
- Bring a polarizing filter to cut glare on the lake surface and deepen the sky during golden hour.
