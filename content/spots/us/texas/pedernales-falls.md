# Pedernales Falls State Park

Pedernales Falls features a dramatic series of cascades where the Pedernales River tumbles over tilted layers of limestone, creating a staircase-like flow pattern that extends hundreds of feet across the riverbed. The east-facing orientation of the main falls area catches beautiful sunrise and morning golden hour light, which rakes across the textured limestone at low angles and brings out the geological layers.

The exposed limestone riverbed is a photographer's playground, with carved channels, pools, and erosion patterns that create endless foreground interest. Water flow varies from a gentle trickle in dry months to a powerful torrent after heavy rains, offering completely different photographic opportunities depending on conditions.

## Best Compositions

- **Long exposure across the cascades** — Set up a tripod on the limestone shelf and use a 16-35mm at f/16 with a 2-8 second exposure (ND filter required in daylight) to render the water as smooth silk flowing over the stepped rock.
- **Sunrise light on the limestone layers** — Position yourself on the east-facing section to catch first light illuminating the tilted limestone strata. Use a 24-70mm at f/11 with the warm light emphasizing the rock texture.
- **Intimate water details** — Use a 70-200mm at f/8 to isolate individual cascades and swirling pools, combining motion blur in the water with sharp rock detail.
- **Wide downstream view from the overlook** — The trail overlook provides a sweeping view down the river corridor. Shoot with a 24mm at f/11 to capture the scale of the falls system.

## Tips

- The riverbed is slippery when wet. Wear water shoes or boots with good grip when scrambling across the limestone to reach shooting positions.
- Bring a 6-stop or 10-stop ND filter for long exposures during daylight hours. Without it, you cannot achieve smooth water effects in bright conditions.
- Flash flooding is a real danger here. Never shoot from the riverbed if rain is forecast upstream, and heed all park warning signs.
