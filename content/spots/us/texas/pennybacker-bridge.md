# Pennybacker Bridge (360 Bridge)

The Pennybacker Bridge is Austin's most iconic photographic landmark, a rust-colored steel arch bridge spanning Lake Austin along Loop 360. The classic overlook shot from the limestone cliffs above the north end of the bridge captures the sweeping arch with the lake below and downtown Austin skyline in the distance. The bridge's warm, oxidized color harmonizes beautifully with golden hour light.

The south-facing overlook means afternoon and evening light illuminates the bridge and surrounding cliffs perfectly. In fall, the surrounding hillsides show touches of color from sumac and other native plants, adding warm tones to complement the bridge's natural patina.

## Best Compositions

- **Classic overlook with downtown skyline** — From the cliff-top trail, use a 24-70mm at f/8 to frame the bridge arc with the distant skyline. A 70-200mm at 100-135mm tightens the composition to emphasize the bridge-skyline relationship.
- **Bridge arc reflection in Lake Austin** — From the waterline below, shoot upward with a 16-35mm at f/11 to capture the full arch reflected in calm water during windless evenings.
- **Traffic light trails at blue hour** — Mount your camera on a tripod and shoot 15-30 second exposures at f/11, ISO 100 to capture red and white light trails streaming across the bridge after sunset.
- **Telephoto compression from the south bank** — Use a 200-400mm from the south side of the lake to compress the bridge against the downtown skyline, stacking the layers dramatically.

## Tips

- The overlook trail is a short but steep scramble from the roadside pullout. Wear sturdy shoes and be cautious near cliff edges, especially while carrying gear.
- Parking along 360 is extremely limited and sometimes restricted. Arrive early on weekends or consider rideshare to avoid parking frustration.
- The best golden hour light hits the bridge face from late afternoon; plan to be set up at least an hour before sunset.
