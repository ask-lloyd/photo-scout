# Hamilton Pool Preserve

Hamilton Pool is a stunning natural swimming hole formed when the dome of an underground river collapsed thousands of years ago, creating a jade-green pool beneath a 50-foot waterfall and dramatic limestone overhang. The grotto faces roughly northeast, and the waterfall cascades over a fern-covered rock ledge into the pool below, creating one of the most photogenic natural features in Texas.

The enclosed canyon setting means light conditions are unique here. Midday sun penetrates the grotto opening, illuminating the waterfall and pool with direct light that brings out the vivid green and turquoise tones of the water. Morning golden hour light filters through the canyon entrance at an angle, creating atmospheric rays through the mist.

## Best Compositions

- **Waterfall with the limestone overhang** — Use a 16-35mm at f/11 from the pool's edge to capture the full scale of the grotto, including the curved rock ceiling and hanging ferns. A 1-2 second exposure smooths the waterfall.
- **Emerald pool detail** — Shoot down into the clear water with a 24-70mm at f/8, using a circular polarizer to cut surface reflections and reveal the submerged limestone floor.
- **Fern-covered cliff face** — Isolate the lush fern colonies clinging to the wet limestone with a 70-200mm at f/4-5.6 for a tight nature detail shot.
- **Wide establishing shot from the trail** — As you descend the trail, there is a viewpoint that reveals the full pool and grotto. Use a 24mm at f/11 to capture the scene with hikers for scale.

## Tips

- Reservations are mandatory and sell out quickly, especially in summer. Book through the Travis County Parks website as soon as slots open.
- Water flow varies dramatically by season and recent rainfall. Check conditions before visiting if the waterfall is your primary subject.
- A circular polarizer is essential here to manage reflections on the water surface and wet rock faces.
