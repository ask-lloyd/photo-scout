# Bob Wentz Park at Windy Point

Bob Wentz Park sits on the western shore of Lake Travis, offering sweeping views across the water toward the Hill Country horizon. Known locally as Windy Point, the park is a favorite among kitesurfers and windsurfers, which gives photographers dynamic foreground subjects against dramatic sunset backdrops.

The rocky limestone shoreline provides natural leading lines into the lake, and the west-facing orientation makes this one of the best sunset locations in the Austin metro area. Water levels fluctuate seasonally, revealing interesting rock formations and textures when the lake is lower.

## Best Compositions

- **Kitesurfer silhouettes at sunset** — Use a 70-200mm to isolate individual kitesurfers against the orange sky, shooting at f/5.6 for sharp action with a blurred background.
- **Wide panoramic across Lake Travis** — Set up with a 16-35mm at f/11, capturing the full expanse of the lake with limestone rocks as foreground interest.
- **Reflection shots from the rocky shore** — During calm evenings, use a 24-70mm at f/8 to capture mirror-like reflections in the shallow pools among the rocks.
- **Sun star through the trees** — Position yourself so the setting sun peeks through lakeside cypress trees; shoot at f/16 with a wide-angle lens to create a clean sun star.

## Tips

- Arrive at least 90 minutes before sunset to scout compositions and secure a good position along the shoreline.
- Water levels in Lake Travis vary significantly; check LCRA water level reports before visiting, as lower levels expose more interesting rock textures.
- The park can get windy (hence the name), so bring a sturdy tripod and consider a remote shutter release to avoid camera shake during longer exposures.
