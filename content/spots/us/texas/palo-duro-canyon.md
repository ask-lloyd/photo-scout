# Palo Duro Canyon State Park

Palo Duro Canyon is the second-largest canyon in the United States, stretching 120 miles long and reaching depths of 800 feet. The exposed rock layers display vivid bands of red, orange, yellow, and white sedimentary formations that glow intensely during golden hour light. The canyon rim offers south-facing panoramic views into the vast chasm, while trails descend to the canyon floor for intimate perspectives among the hoodoos and formations.

The Lighthouse formation is the park's most iconic feature, a towering rock pillar that makes a compelling photographic subject at any time of day. The canyon's east-west orientation means both sunrise and sunset light penetrate deep into the gorge, painting the red rock walls with warm, saturated color that shifts minute by minute.

## Best Compositions

- **The Lighthouse formation at golden hour** — Hike the 6-mile round-trip Lighthouse Trail and shoot with a 24-70mm at f/8. Position yourself to catch the formation backlit at sunset or front-lit at sunrise for maximum color saturation.
- **Canyon rim panorama** — From the rim overlooks along the park road, shoot a multi-frame panorama with a 35-50mm at f/11 to capture the full sweep of layered red rock stretching to the horizon.
- **Layered rock detail** — Use a 70-200mm at f/8 from the canyon rim to compress the colorful sedimentary bands into abstract patterns of red, orange, and cream.
- **Hoodoo formations from the canyon floor** — Descend to the canyon bottom and shoot upward with a 16-35mm at f/11 to exaggerate the height of eroded pillars and balanced rocks against the sky.

## Tips

- The Lighthouse Trail hike takes 3-4 hours round trip. Start early enough to reach the formation before golden hour, and carry a headlamp for the return hike in darkness.
- The canyon rim is at 3,500 feet in the Texas Panhandle. Winter mornings can be bitterly cold and windy; dress in layers and bring gloves that allow camera operation.
- Check for road closures after heavy rain, as flash flooding can temporarily close canyon floor trails and the park road.
